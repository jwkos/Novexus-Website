What is KLU?

An AI that knows how parts connect

KLU turns intent ("stack two boxes", "mount bracket to panel") into precise assembly plans. It proposes placements, then validates them using a CAD kernel — avoiding floating parts and enforcing realistic connections.

Spatial planning

Understands scene layout and available faces before placing parts. Plans holistically rather than step‑by‑step.

Connection logic

Face/edge/point awareness with snapping and mating rules. Designed to prevent overlap and enforce constraints.

CAD‑first outputs

STEP is the ground truth for accuracy. Web previews leverage GLB for speed; exports target real assemblies.

Architecture

Precise by design

  • Interpreter maps natural language to structured part actions.
  • Parts resolved from a catalog (STEP). Geometry informs constraints.
  • Validation checks snapping, floating elements, and collision/overlap.
  • Autoregressive revision fixes flagged placements before output.
